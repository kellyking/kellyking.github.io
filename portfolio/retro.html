UX Retro & Brainstorm Session

Overview

This past summer our entire ecommerce group met in person for a week-long get together/conference. I was asked to colead a group of 30+ coworkers, with the goal of combining knowledge from multiple disciplines to generate ideas about how Shane Co could be a more customer-centric company.

Skills

Workshops

Tools

Figjam

Homework

Before we got together, everyone was given a list of competitors to analyze. We focused our analyses on several ecommerce strategies: personlization, loyalty, brand differentiation, and driving urgency. The second part to the homework was to focus on their own interactions with our customers, to identify customer pain/happy points. The goal of the homework was to generate as many ideas and experiences as possible, without trying to find solutions.

Session Intro

Our session was only scheduled for 2 and half hours, and we had a lot to accomplish. I projected a slide of our schedule to keep us on track and then explained our goal, tasks, and rules. For the first 30 minutes we individually wrote down ideas from our homework onto post-its and organized them using the Glad, Mad, Sad retro format.

Retrospectives

We then ran 3 sets of micro-retrospectives. With the first set, coworkers were paired up and worked together to decide between them which 3-4 of their ideas is most valuable. For the 2nd set, two pairs then formed a group of 4 and completed the same activity, whittling down the ideas to 3-4. With the 3rd set, two groups of 4 then formed a group of 8 and completed the same activity. Each group of 8 then shared with everyone their chosen ideas. We clustered similar ideas and ranked the top ones. We discussed possible solutions to these ideas and how we, as a team, could implement them.

Results

After our session, I worked to transcribe all of our ideas and results into a Figjam document, so that we could reference later on. Two of our top ideas have been included in next years development plan.
