Redesign Mobile App

Overview

Our customers used our app to watch/listen/read the media they purchased. My task was to analyze the customer feedback (from the App and Google Play store) and make recommendations for overall design, layout, user interactions, and user flows. I also assisted with the accessibility review of the final app.

Research

Fortunately, we have several hundred app reviews for both our iOS and Android apps. I read through and coded the qualitative data in order to quantify and understand what our customers thought about our apps. Overall, customers were initially happy when the apps first came out. However, due to the lack of regular updates and enhancements, they became disappointed in the apps lack of features that are commonly found in similar applications, such as iTunes, Netflix, Amazon Prime, Audible, etc.

Solution

Unfortunately, the need to ship a MVP quickly restricted the inclusion of some functionality. However, we were able to incorporate our top customer requests. For example, customers will be able to read an ebook, watch a video, and listen to an audio file directly within the app.

User Tests

I ran one round of in-person paper prototype user tests using my initial low-fidelity wireframes. These tests resulted in several minor changes to the designs, for example changing a heading on one of the pages.

I then collaborated with our Digital Designer on the high-fidelity mock-ups. Using the mock-ups, I ran a round of remote interactive prototype tests. These user tests produced positive results: no major usability issues were found.

Once the developers completed building the new app, I ran a beta test with my fellow co-workers (100+). The test consisted of users completing multiple end-to-end tasks and completing a survey.

Results

All the edits were made from the uer tests, and the app has a successful launched. The app held a steady 6% conversion rate for purchases.
