PDP ABn Test

Overview

After a new PDP (product detail page) design was deployed, we were disappointed in its performance. We hypothesized that one of the reasons for the low conversion was the amount of scrolling required to reach the main CTA. The main CTA was quite a ways down the page: 2 full page scrolls on desktop and 4 on mobile.

Testing

The goal of the ABn test was to see if compressing the page and moving up the main CTA would help customers continue their shopping journey. We ran the test only on a subset of PDPs - pre-built enagagement rings. There were three different variants in the test:

  1. Original: no changes made to the page.
  2. Variant B: page is compressed. (The main CTA was slightly below the average fold on desktop.)
  3. Variant C: page is compressed and the main CTA was moved above a Preview Options section. (The main CTA is above the average fold on desktop.)

To modify the PDP layout for the variants, I added custom CSS and jQuery to Google Optmize to run the test. The test was for all customers on all device types, and was set to equal distribution across the 3 variants. Our determining metric was clicks to the main CTA. We also monitored PDP revenue and number of transactions.

Results

We only needed to run the test for 7 days before generating statistically relevant data. Variant B had a statistically significant improvement in both clicks to the main CTA and unique product purchases. In addition, the two new variants contributed an additional 6-figures in product revenue during these 7 days.

Next Steps

We deployed the winning variant to 100% of the subset of PDPs and began the process of updating all the PDP templates to include this compressed style.
