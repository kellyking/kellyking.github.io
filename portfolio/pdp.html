PDP Optimization A/B/n Test (Ecommerce Conversion)

  • Role: Lead UX Researcher
  • Scope: Product Detail Page (PDP) optimization
  • Method: A/B/n experimentation
  • Business Impact: 90.5% increase in product revenue on tested PDPs

Context

Following the launch of a redesigned Product Detail Page (PDP), performance fell short of expectations. Initial analytics indicated lower-than-anticipated conversion rates, prompting a deeper investigation into potential usability and interaction issues.

Based on behavioral data and UX heuristics, I hypothesized that excessive page length and the placement of the primary call-to-action (CTA) were creating frictionâ€”particularly on mobile, where users were required to scroll multiple times before encountering the primary action.

Research Question & Hypothesis

Research question: Does reducing PDP length and surfacing the primary CTA earlier in the page hierarchy increase engagement and conversion?

Hypothesis: Compressing the PDP layout and repositioning the primary CTA closer to the top of the page would reduce friction, increase clicks to the CTA, and ultimately improve conversion and revenue.

Experiment Design

To validate the hypothesis, I designed and executed an A/B/n test focused on a high-intent subset of PDPs: pre-built engagement rings.

Variants tested:

  • Control (Original): Existing PDP design with no changes
  • Variant B: Compressed layout with the primary CTA positioned slightly below the average desktop fold
  • Variant C: Further compressed layout with the primary CTA moved above the Preview Options section (above the average desktop fold)

The experiment ran across all device types with equal traffic distribution to each variant.

Execution & Measurement

To implement the test, I created custom CSS and jQuery within Google Optimize to dynamically modify the PDP layout by variant.

Primary success metric: Clicks to the primary CTA. Secondary metrics: Unique product purchases, PDP revenue, Number of transactions. This metric hierarchy ensured we measured both behavioral intent and downstream business impact.

Results & Impact

After just seven days, the test reached statistical significance.

Variant B demonstrated a statistically significant increase in:

  • Clicks to the primary CTA
  • Unique product purchases

Across both experimental variants, the test generated an additional six figures in incremental product revenue during the test window Overall, the winning design drove a 90.5% increase in product revenue on the tested PDPs.

Decision & Next Steps

Based on the results, we deployed the winning variant to 100% of the targeted PDP subset. I then partnered with design and engineering to begin updating all PDP templates to incorporate the compressed layout and improved CTA placement at scale.

Lead Researcher Contribution

  • Identified conversion friction using behavioral analysis and UX principles
  • Designed and executed a multi-variant experiment tied to revenue outcomes
  • Defined success metrics and ensured statistical rigor
  • Influenced product and design direction through clear, data-backed recommendations
