Checkout Redesign & Optimization (Ecommerce)

  • Role: Lead UX Researcher
  • Scope: End-to-end checkout experience
  • Methods: Quantitative & qualitative analysis, remote usability testing, competitive benchmarking, A/B testing
  • Impact: Achieved steady double-digit increase in checkout completion rates

Context

The checkout process is a critical conversion point for ecommerce. Early data indicated friction in our one-page checkout, contributing to lower-than-expected completion rates. I led a year-long research and redesign initiative to optimize the checkout flow, improve usability, and increase conversion.

This project required coordination across multiple teams: ecommerce, design, development, and QA, ensuring research insights were implemented effectively in production.

Problem Identification

Initial analysis revealed key issues in the existing checkout:

  • Low completion rates and high exit rates
  • Inefficient form design and unclear user flows
  • Friction points identified via click maps and session data

Benchmarking against best practices from the Baymard Institute confirmed that a stepped checkout flow would reduce cognitive load and improve task completion compared to the existing single-page layout.

Research & Insights

I designed a multi-method research plan to guide the redesign:

  • Quantitative analysis: GA4 data, click maps, form analytics, and funnel completion rates
  • Qualitative insights: Remote usability tests of interactive prototypes
  • Competitive and best-practice research: Evaluated top ecommerce sites and industry guidelines to inform layout and flow improvements

Key insight: while simplifying headers and footers is generally recommended, AB testing revealed that this change negatively impacted completion rates, leading us to remove it from the final design.

Solution & Iteration

  • Developed wireframes and user flows based on research insights
  • Created interactive HTML/CSS/jQuery prototypes for remote testing
  • Collaborated across design, development, and QA teams, iterating on flows based on user feedback
  • Presented a 4-slide proposal to ecommerce and design leadership to gain approval for development
  • Delivered a Creative Brief including wireframes, flows, and development notes to ensure design intent was implemented accurately

Results & Impact

Post-launch metrics show significant improvements:

  • Double-digit increase in checkout completion rates
  • Reduced exit rates across multiple checkout steps
  • Streamlined multi-step checkout flow that aligns with user expectations and industry best practices

The redesign demonstrates how research-driven design and iterative validation can directly influence conversion metrics and overall ecommerce performance.

Lead Researcher Contribution

  • Conducted usability testing and A/B experiments to validate design decisions
  • Synthesized research into wireframes, prototypes, and creative briefs to guide development
  • Tracked post-launch metrics to measure impact and validate success
