Redesign Checkout

Overview

I developed a research project plan for the redesign of checkout on our ecom website. This plan consisted of multiple different research methods including both quant and qual data. I worked with the ecom team, designers, developers, and the QA team to complete this project. This project lasted about 1 year, from initial research to deployment to production website.

Research

The beginning of the research plan consisted of analyzing existing quant data, such as completion rates, user flows, click maps, and form completion analysis. I also researched checkout best-practices on Baymard Institute, a leader in UX research. From all this data, it was clear that we needed to change from a one-page checkout to a stepped checkout.

I created wireframes and user flows and went through several rounds of presentations and collaboration with my ecom teammates to finalize the details. I created an interactive prototype using html/css/jQuery and ran a series of remote user tests. The results from these tests were promising.

There was one major design change we proposed for the new checkout, that was recommended by Baymard Institute - simplifying the header and footer on the checkout page. We decided to AB tested this change to determine if it had any negative effects. Unfortunately, it had a huge negative effect on our checkout completion rates. We removed this design change from our proposal.

I created a short 4-slide presentation that illustrated the changes in page layout and design, and presented this to the ecom lead and the design lead. The ecom lead then shared our proposal with the owner, we ultimately got the go-ahead to begin development.

I wrote a Creative Brief that included my wireframes, user flows, and important notes for the development team. I presented this brief to the designers, design managers, creative lead, ecom lead, and dev lead.

Once the development started in our QA environment, I assited with the testing of the new design. When it was released in production, I tracked metrics including conversion rate, checkout completion, and exit rate. The new checkout has had a steady double-digit percent increase in completion rate since it went live.
